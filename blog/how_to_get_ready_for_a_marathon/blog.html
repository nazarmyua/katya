How to get ready for a marathon

How to get ready for a marathon

Getting ready for a marathon takes a lot of time, energy and commitment. We’re gathered the essential information to support you in this long journey.

Decide on the start date

Keep in mind that you need to start you preparation minimum 5 month prior to the race. It doesn’t matter whether you a beginner or experienced runner, marathon is something you should take very seriously. For beginners we even recommend doing a half marathon or even a 10k as your first official competition, just to get familiar with the atmosphere, adapt to the environment of running with thousands of opponents and work on the strategy during an actual competition. You can easily find a local race in your city, the more practice – the more comfortable you will feel for the actual marathon run.

Preparation

Evaluate how much time you are willing to dedicate for your training course: preparation will take a lot of commitment: at least up to 3 hours almost every day. Ideally, you should take at least couple of lessons with the trainer. If that is not possible for some reason, we still recommend doing some background research and make a training plan using books, video blogs on running and online services. Try to get familiar with chosen race and start doing you preparation in similar climate, relief, road surface, time of day. Which leads us to a next recommendation: do not choose something exotic for your first race; it will be hard for you to adjust during an actual run.

If you don’t have serious ambitions for a time goal do not focus on speed rather on endurance training: always do at least one long training per week (for example, on Sunday), after that you can rest and even take a day off on Monday. You should also devote some time to a strength training: train your legs, body and arms, do a push-ups, a plank, do exercises on the horizontal and parallel bars. This will secure you from unnecessary injuries.

Equipment and nutrition

Test your equipment, shoes and clothes to eventually pick something you’re 100% comfortable with. This advice is also applicable to your nutrition: try on energy bars and gels, dried and fresh fruits to figure out what you and your stomach tolerate the most. It’s also important to attempt different nutrition tactics during a run: the faster you run the faster your body burns through available energy, so figure out your average speed, and think about how often you need to grab a bite. In addition, remember to start drinking fluids early, don’t wait until you’re thirsty. You should also practice eating and drinking while running to keep you on your rhythm while fueling up. Which leads us to a next recommendation: do not choose something exotic for your first race; it will be hard for you to adjust during an actual run.

Control training before a marathon race

There should be at least two main control training during your preparation course: run a half marathon at least 4 to 5 weeks before the main race in a very quiet rhythm – just to cover the distance. Approximately 3 weeks prior to the marathon, you should significantly reduce the training pace to give your body some rest. Your second control training should be a distance aerobic exercise, again in a very soft acceptable rhythm. Last week before the marathon, it’s recommended doing a short (5-10 km) runs to maintain gained shape before the main start and give your muscles some time to recover and accumulate maximum amount of glycogen for the day of start.

Motivation

Your course of preparation should be comfortable for you. The minute you start forcing yourself to training you start losing a motivation. Listen you your body and what it’s trying to tell you. When you hit the point you can’t overcome by yourself get some perspective from a trainer or research some recommendation from professional runners. You may also want to read some inspiring literature on psychology, because not only your body, but your mind also needs to be prepared for the big day.

The marathon is very unique and a tough race. Preparation to such challenge takes a lot of energy and commitment. We hope that during this journey you will not only appear stronger, but also find something new about yourself and become an inspiration to a hundreds of people around you.
