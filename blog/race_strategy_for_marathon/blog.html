Race strategy for a marathon

Race strategy for a marathon

Your preparation is done and the big day has come. Don’t worry, we got your back! Here are some tips and advices to help you get through marathon distance

Equipment

Running shoes and other equipment should match perfectly to the individual anatomical features of your body, so you won’t feel any discomfort during a few hours of running. Newer wear a new pair of shoes or unfamiliar equipment on the day of the race.

Preparation

To avoid unpleasant surprises during the marathon, we recommend you to get familiar with the track and the characteristics of its relief in advance. Where and which side are aid stations, what are the track features. Mind the inclinations and their length. Think about your strategy, decide where you can save your energy and where to accelerate. The route of the marathon can run through a variety of places - streets, fields, forests, hills. Road surface is also very important. It’s better to walk or jog along the route in advance, but if this is not possible - you can always explore the map and the route profile on marathonized.com.

Set Appropriate Time Goals

Think about your time goal in advance. It’s always better to have plan B if something goes wrong during the race. This way you can easily switch to the slower pace and continue the distance without any confusion or psychological discomfort. Try not to pressure yourself with unrealistic time limit in case you’ve never showed similar results during your marathon training course. Get your numbers straight and you will feel much more comfortable along the distance. You can use your previous results, pace calculators or information about past marathons as a reference.

Simply finish the race is also an honorable goal if you are running your first marathon. Pick your pace, run within your limits; this way you can be sure you can make it to the finish line.

Start

It’s really easy to get a little bit over excited with the atmosphere and euphoria on the start. Try to calm yourself down and remember your distance plan. You may get confused with the big crowd at the start; it may be hard to keep to the chosen rhythm from the beginning of the race. So, try to go with the flow at first: it’s okay to run at slower pace during the start, at the end of the day, it’s the whole distance that matters

The first 5 miles

Do not try to compete with other runners, and jump over your head at the beginning of the race. It’s likely that you will waste your energy an show a lot worse result than you could’ve sticking to the chosen pace.

If you don’t have serious ambitions for a time goal do not focus on speed rather on endurance training: always do at least one long training per week (for example, on Sunday), after that you can rest and even take a day off on Monday. You should also devote some time to a strength training: train your legs, body and arms, do a push-ups, a plank, do exercises on the horizontal and parallel bars. This will secure you from unnecessary injuries.

Eating/drinking during the marathon

Aid stations are located on every 5 kilometers during the distance. They offer bananas, dried fruits, candy, cereal bars, as well as drinking water and sweetened beverages. General rule: do not be tempted with unfamiliar food or drinks, stick to your usual routine. The day of the race is not the best time to have such a risky experiment.

While running, do not bring yourself to a state of thirst or hunger, think about a little snack or drink a water in advance: it’s important to maintain your water balance and blood sugar level. We suggest running 50 minutes to one hour before ingesting food/energy gels. This way your body will have some time to catch the rhythm and efficiently process the sugar you’re consuming. Remember to take some water with your food as well.

Second half of the race

The longer the distance, the greater internal resources you’re require to overcome it. You can read special literature and the reviews from marathon runners for inspiration and useful tips. You will need to develop discipline, willpower and ability stay strong in difficult times, because the second half of the race is all about your psychological condition. Try to think positive and concentrate on your goal. Remember your initial motivation to run the marathon and think about all the support you have from your family and friends.

Try to keep the race map in your mind. Remember the details like “the bridge”, “the big tree” – it’s much easier to divide the remaining distance into small pieces and have something to look forward to along the way. It also helps to think about your marathon preparation routine: remember the highlights of your training, think about the challenges you’ve faced and how great it felt to complete each one of those challenges to finally end up running a marathon distance.

Running a marathon is a very special and unique experience. Try to embrace the atmosphere, have fun and enjoy yourself. We hope these tips will help you to focus on the most important things during the race. From all of us here at marathonized.com we wish you the best of luck!
