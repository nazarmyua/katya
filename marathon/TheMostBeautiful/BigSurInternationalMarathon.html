BRIEFLY

The Big Sur International Marathon will be held on April 30, 2017. This boutique-style marathon is considered a “bucket list” race for running enthusiasts and it has earned many accolades including “Best Destination Marathon in North America” by the Ultimate Guide to Marathons. If you are looking to run on all seven continents, this is a great North America option!

This truly is an international marathon, with runners and spectators coming from all over the world to participate in this event, known as one of the TOP-3 marathons in the USA. From Big Sur to Carmel's Rio Road, runners wind through majestic redwoods and along Pacific Ocean, enjoying one of the most beautiful corners of the country.

MAP

HISTORY

The Big Sur International Marathon is an annual marathon held in California, United States along the Pacific coast. The marathon was established in 1986 and attracts about 4,500 participants annually. As the SAN JOSE MERCURY NEWS pronounced in 1986, "...the Big Sur International Marathon crashed onto the race calendar like a blast of fresh air as about 1,800 runners participated in the maiden 26.2 mile trek from Big Sur to Carmel."

INTERESTING FACTS

Drummers

The sound of drums echoing between mountains is a motivating sound for Big Sur International Marathon runners, and also a signal for a huge challenge ahead.

The Taiko drumming group from Watsonville is positioned near the race's half-way mark, at the base of Hurricane Point. Hurricane Point is the steepest, most intimidating, and most grueling hill of the marathon's course.

The strawberry aid station

To help marathon runners fuel up for the race, there is an official” strawberry aid station” at about the 23 mile mark.

Michael Martinez

Along the way you can listen how Michael Martinez is playing his grand piano in full formal tails right on the cliff.

Carmel Highlands

The more experienced runners and reviews had dismissed the big climb at Hurricane Point as the toughest part of the course.

GEOGRAPHY/CLIMATE

The proximity of the ocean modifies temperatures year round, providing a mild climate with minimal variations. Over 300 days a year of sunshine are interspersed with seasonal rains of over fifty inches a year. Cool coastal fog often develops overnight and usually clears by midday in summer. Spring and fall bring the Sur's best weather, with clear, warm days and cool starry nights. Strong wind though can certainly be an issue.
Spring - Daytime temperatures 60-70 F. Rain is much less frequent.

INFORMATION FOR TOURIST

Carmel by the Sea

Spend some time in Carmel-by-the-Sea, a beautiful coastal town located off of Highway One just five miles South of Monterey City. The town itself is very small, but there is plenty to see and do there, including: art galleries, cafes, restaurants and inns. http://www.carmelcalifornia.com/

Monterey Bay Aquarium

Visit the Monterey Bay Aquarium and see more than 600 species they have on display. http://www.montereybayaquarium.org/vi/

Monterey

Experience the culinary delights that Monterey County has to offer. The area has a yearlong growing season and it offers a variety of cuisine to appeal to any palate. Wine connoisseurs will be happy to note that Monterey County was nominated one of the “Top Ten Wine Travel Destinations” in the world by Wine Enthusiast Magazine this year.

Highway One

Go for a ride. Monterey and the surrounding area are known for legendary scenic drives. Here is a list of top drives and maps including Highway One and 17-Mile Drive: Scenic Drives. (It's good to note that gas stations are few and far between and gas can be expensive in the Big Sur region.)
